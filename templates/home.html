Econ Game: Constitution Compromise

Its 1787; 2-7 delegates from each of the 13 colonies are meeting at a privately held convention in Philadelphia known as the Constitutional Convention. The purpose of this meeting is to create a new system of government for the US by drafting a constitution. Groups of 2 people from this class will each represent a state. Because of different demographics - population, slave count, access to certain materials - the states have differing opinions regarding each policy. The goal of this convention is to eventually compromise on 5 key policies: representation, slaves’ right to vote, taxation, trade regulation, and slavery. A description of your state’s condition coming into this meeting is outlined in a blurb. Based on your description, you need to decide on your preferences regarding these policies. Of course, there will be disagreement. In order to create a constitution beneficial to the greater United States, you will need to find compromises. In order for a policy to be passed, everyone needs to agree on the compromise. Some states may benefit more than others regarding certain policies; these states will gain more points. At the end of the game, if a constitution is not decided on, everyone will receive a collective failure. If everyone does decide on a constitution, the states that benefit the most will win.
